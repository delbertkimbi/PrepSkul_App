# How to Find "App Access" in Google Play Console

## 🎯 Quick Navigation Guide

Based on your screenshots, here's exactly where to go:

### **Step 1: Navigate to App Content**

1. In Google Play Console, you're already at: **Policy and programs** → **App content**
2. You see the "Need attention" tab (which shows "You're all caught up")

### **Step 2: Find "App access" Declaration**

The "App access" section is **NOT** in the "Need attention" tab. It's a separate declaration card. Here's how to find it:

**Option A: Scroll Down on the Same Page**
1. On the **"App content"** page, scroll down below the tabs
2. Look for declaration cards/sections like:
   - Privacy policy
   - **App access** ← This is what you need!
   - Data safety
   - Content ratings
   - etc.

**Option B: Use the Direct Link**
1. Click on this link in the "How to fix" page: **"app access"** (it's a blue clickable link)
2. This should take you directly to the App access declaration

**Option C: Search for It**
1. On the "App content" page, look for a search bar or filter
2. Type "app access" or "login credentials"
3. Click on the "App access" result

### **Step 3: What You Should See**

When you find "App access", you should see:
- A card/section titled **"App access"** or **"App access declaration"**
- A question like: "Does your app require users to sign in or register?"
- Options like:
  - "All functionality is available without login"
  - "Some or all functionality is restricted" ← **Select this one**
- A section to add login credentials/instructions

### **Step 4: Add Demo Account Credentials**

Once you're in the "App access" section:

1. **Select:** "Some or all functionality is restricted"
2. **Click:** "Add new instructions" or "Edit" or "Manage"
3. **Fill in the form:**
   - **Email/Username:** `demo@prepskul.com` (or your demo email)
   - **Password:** `PrepSkulDemo2026!` (or your demo password)
   - **Instructions:** (See template below)
4. **Save** the credentials

### **Step 5: Instructions Template**

Copy and paste this into the "Instructions" field:

```
Demo Account Credentials for PrepSkul Review:

Email: demo@prepskul.com
Password: PrepSkulDemo2026!

Account Type: Pre-approved Tutor Account

IMPORTANT NOTES FOR REVIEWERS:
- This account is already approved and has full access to all features
- No waiting period or manual approval needed
- You can immediately access:
  * Tutor dashboard
  * Student booking management
  * Session scheduling and management
  * Profile and settings
  * Video call features (Agora)
  * All app functionality

The account has completed all onboarding steps and is ready for review.

If you encounter any issues accessing the account, please contact: support@prepskul.com
```

## 🔍 If You Still Can't Find It

### **Alternative Method 1: Use the Direct Link from "How to fix" Page**

1. Go to: **Policy status** → Click on the rejected issue
2. Click **"How to fix"** tab
3. Click the blue link that says **"app access"** 
4. This should take you directly to the App access declaration

### **Alternative Method 2: Check "Actioned" Tab**

1. On the **"App content"** page
2. Click the **"Actioned"** tab (instead of "Need attention")
3. Look for "App access" in the completed declarations
4. Click on it to edit/add credentials

### **Alternative Method 3: Search in Play Console**

1. Use the search bar at the top of Play Console
2. Type: "app access"
3. Click on the result that says "App access declaration"

## 📋 Do You Need to Upload a New App Bundle?

### **Answer: NO, you don't need a new bundle for this issue!**

**Why:**
- The "Missing demo account" issue is a **Play Console configuration** issue
- It's NOT a code issue
- You just need to add credentials in the Play Console settings
- No app bundle upload required

**When you DO need a new bundle:**
- Only if you made code changes (like removing permissions, fixing bugs, etc.)
- For the current issue, just update the Play Console settings

## ✅ What to Do After Adding Credentials

1. **Save** the credentials in App access
2. **Go to:** Publishing overview
3. **Click:** "Send changes for review" or "Submit for review"
4. **Wait:** 1-3 business days for review

## 🆘 Still Can't Find It?

If you absolutely cannot find the "App access" section, try:

1. **Check if it's already completed:**
   - Go to "App content" → "Actioned" tab
   - Look for "App access" there
   - If found, click to edit it

2. **Contact Google Play Support:**
   - In Play Console, go to Help → Contact us
   - Explain you can't find the "App access" declaration
   - They can guide you or enable it if it's missing

3. **Check your app's eligibility:**
   - Some app types might have different requirements
   - Make sure your app is set up correctly in Play Console
