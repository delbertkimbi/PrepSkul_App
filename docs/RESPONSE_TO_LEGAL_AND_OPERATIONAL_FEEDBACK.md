# Response to Legal & Operational Feedback on Onsite Safety

**Context:** After sharing our first version of the onsite pitch (before the global-platforms analysis), we received strong feedback: the product accountability layer is solid, but **real-world risk requires three layers—Prevention, Real-time control, Post-incident protection—and we need stronger prevention and legal shields.** This document maps that feedback to what we have, what we’re missing, and what we should add. It also addresses **NDAs**, **marketplace vs employer**, and **honest answers** (e.g. background checks).

---

## 1. Did we consider NDAs and “this stuff”?

**Short answer:** We had not explicitly called out NDAs, legal framing, safeguarding policies, or insurance in the pitch. The feedback is right: **we were strong on tech and process, light on legal and operational framing.**

**What we do now:**

- **NDAs:** When sharing the pitch or sensitive product/ops details with investors or partners, use an NDA where appropriate (e.g. before sharing detailed safety architecture or financials). The **pitch document itself** can be shared under a simple confidentiality expectation or NDA depending on the counterparty. We don’t need to describe NDA flow inside the pitch; we need a **team rule**: “Pitch and safety docs: share under NDA when the other side expects it or when we’re sharing beyond high-level.”
- **Legal and operational “stuff”:** We now treat them as part of the story. Below we add: **Vetting & Onboarding**, **Safeguarding Policy**, **Legal Protection**, and **Insurance** as the four pillars that sit alongside our tech controls. We also add **honest positioning** (marketplace vs employer, what we do today vs what we’re adding).

---

## 2. Three layers of risk: where we are

| Layer | What it means | What we have | What we’re missing or should add |
|-------|----------------|---------------|-----------------------------------|
| **1. Prevention** | Reduce opportunity and set expectations before harm | Tutor ID + credentials at onboarding; optional parent/learner KYC for first onsite. In-app guidance (e.g. adult present or reachable). | **Mandatory** background screening (or clear “we don’t yet” + roadmap). **Signed** code of conduct and **child safeguarding policy**. **Parent presence policy** and **physical boundaries** (visible area, no locked room with minor) in writing and signed. |
| **2. Real-time control** | Know what’s happening during the session | Check-in at venue, location sharing, continuous location checks, “Report issue” → safety incident + admin alert. | Strong. We can add explicit in-app reminders of safeguarding rules (e.g. “Sessions must be in a visible area; parent/guardian should be present or reachable”). |
| **3. Post-incident protection** | Respond, protect, and limit liability after something goes wrong | Safety incidents, resolution notes, session timeline, risk score. We don’t force tutor back into unsafe household; we can suspend/bar. Dispute review with evidence. | **Written** escalation protocol, **zero tolerance** and **blacklist** policies. **Legal**: Terms of Service, independent contractor agreement, indemnity, limitation of liability, incident process. **Insurance**: state what we have or intent to secure (e.g. professional indemnity as we scale). |

So: **our approach is strong on layers 2 and 3 (tech and process); we can learn a lot from the exceptions mentioned** by adding prevention (policies, signing, background checks or clear stance) and legal/insurance.

## 3. Learning from the exceptions mentioned

We adopt the following from the feedback.

### Tutor misconduct with minors

- **We do not claim tech prevents abuse.** We say we **reduce risk** through verification, location checks, reporting, and evidence. Technology confirms presence and proximity; it does not prevent misconduct inside a private home.
- **Add (and document):**
  - **Safeguarding policy** that includes: parent/guardian presence or reachable for minors; sessions in **visible areas** of the home; **no tutor alone in a locked room** with a minor; **no physical contact** beyond acceptable professional norms.
  - **Code of conduct** that tutors (and optionally parents) **sign** at onboarding or before first onsite.
  - **Parent presence policy** in writing (e.g. “For learners under 18, a parent or guardian must be present at home or immediately reachable during onsite sessions”).
- **Background screening:** The feedback asks for “mandatory background screening.” Today we do **ID and academic credential verification**, not criminal/background checks. We should **answer honestly**: “We verify identity and credentials today; we are [adding / evaluating] background checks for tutors working with minors.” If we’re not doing them yet, say so and state intent (e.g. “We are evaluating partner providers for background checks as we scale”).

### Parent harassment of tutor

- **We have:** Clear reporting (safety incident), we don’t force tutor back, we can suspend/bar the family.
- **Add in writing:**
  - **Zero tolerance harassment policy** (short, clear: harassment of tutors or learners is not tolerated; we will suspend or bar and support the affected party).
  - **Escalation protocol:** who is notified, when we suspend a session or booking, when we blacklist, and how we support the tutor (e.g. offer to reassign, document for their records).
  - **Blacklist protocol:** how we record and enforce (e.g. no future bookings from that household / for that tutor).

### Legal liability

- **Clarify positioning:** We are a **marketplace** connecting independent tutors and parents. We are **not** the employer of tutors. That must be clear in **Terms of Service** and in **contractor agreements** with tutors.
- **Add or reinforce in legal docs:**
  - **Independent contractor clause** (tutors are independent; we do not direct how they teach; we facilitate the match and the platform).
  - **Indemnity** (user indemnifies us for their conduct where permitted by law).
  - **Limitation of liability** (cap or exclusion where enforceable).
  - **Safeguarding policy** referenced in Terms (and optionally a separate policy that users accept).
- **Incident liability process:** What we do when something is reported (gather facts, support victim, suspend if needed, cooperate with authorities, document; we do not admit liability in the process).

### Insurance

- **State clearly:** What we have today (if any): e.g. “We carry [X]” or “We do not yet carry professional indemnity.”
- **Intent:** “As we scale, we intend to secure appropriate insurance (e.g. professional indemnity) for platform operations.”

### Language: don’t overpromise

- **Avoid:** “We prevent abuse.”
- **Use instead:** “We **reduce risk** through **structured safeguards** and **documented accountability**.” Or: “We reduce opportunity, give everyone a voice, create evidence, and support escalation—we don’t claim to eliminate all risk.”

We’ll use this language in the pitch and in policy.

## 5. Marketplace vs managed service

**Feedback:** “Are you building a marketplace model long-term or a managed service? Safety architecture depends on that.”

**Recommended position for PrepSkul:** We are a **marketplace** that connects independent tutors with families. We provide the platform, verification, safety tools, and policies; we do **not** employ tutors or direct how they teach. That keeps liability and employment law on the right side and scales better. Our safety architecture (verification, code of conduct, safeguarding policy, incident response, legal terms) supports that: we set rules and tools, we don’t guarantee tutor conduct.

**If we ever offer “managed” or “premium” tiers** where we take more responsibility (e.g. employed tutors, guaranteed quality), we would need different legal and insurance setup. For the current pitch and product, **marketplace** is the right frame.

---

## 6. Background checks: answer honestly

**Feedback:** “Are you currently doing background checks on tutors? Answer honestly.”

**We should:**

- **If we do:** Say clearly: “We run [e.g. criminal record / identity] checks on tutors through [provider] before approval.”
- **If we don’t:** Say: “We currently verify tutor identity and academic credentials; we do not yet run criminal background checks. We are [evaluating providers / planning to introduce background checks for tutors working with minors] as we scale.” Then add it to the **Vetting & onboarding** pillar and roadmap.

Honesty in the pitch and in due diligence is better than overclaiming. Investors will respect a clear “here’s what we do today, here’s what we’re adding.”

---

## 7. NDAs and confidentiality

- **When sharing the pitch or safety docs** with investors, partners, or judges: use an NDA or confidentiality agreement when the situation requires it (e.g. before sharing detailed architecture, financials, or roadmaps). The pitch doc can include a short line: “Confidential – for use in connection with [pitch / partnership] discussions.”
- **In contractor/tutor agreements:** Include confidentiality (and if relevant, non-disclosure) so tutors don’t share our internal processes or safety playbooks inappropriately. Standard practice for marketplaces.
- **In Terms of Service:** Users agree to keep certain platform and safety processes confidential where we specify (e.g. fraud or safety investigation procedures). Don’t overreach; focus on what’s necessary to protect the platform and users.

---

## 8. Summary: is our approach better or do we learn?

- **Our approach (tech + accountability + evidence)** is strong for **real-time control** and **post-incident protection** and is good product thinking. It is **not enough alone** for real-world risk.
- **We learn from the exceptions mentioned:** Add **prevention** (safeguarding policy, code of conduct, parent presence policy, physical boundaries, and background checks or clear roadmap). Add **legal protection** (marketplace positioning, ToS, contractor agreement, indemnity, limitation of liability). Add **written policies** (zero tolerance, blacklist, escalation). Add **insurance** (current state + intent). Tone down **“prevent”** to **“reduce risk through structured safeguards and documented accountability.”**
- **NDAs:** Use them when sharing pitch/safety details with investors or partners where appropriate; cover confidentiality in contractor and user terms where relevant.
