THE PROBLEM

Students have notes, classes, and exams — but revision is passive, generic, and
disconnected from how they actually learn. Parents pay for extra lessons but
can't see whether their child is improving on the right things. Tutors spend
time on repetition that software could handle, and not enough on real
misconceptions.

THE INSIGHT

"Form 5 Chemistry" is a label, not a capability profile. Two learners in the
same class can need completely different paths. One needs exam drill. Another
needs foundations. Another needs motivation and structure. Treating them the
same fails everyone.

WHAT PREPSKUL IS

PrepSkul is a learning platform that meets every learner at their level and
guides them to their goals with:

  1. SkulMate — AI revision that turns uploads, notes, YouTube, and class
     sessions into games and practice matched to the learner's context.

  2. Tutor marketplace — verified tutors for live online and in-person sessions
     when human intervention is the right tool.

  3. Adaptive layer — mastery tracking, weak-topic detection, and rerouting so
     the path updates as the learner improves — not a static content bucket
     from signup day.

WHO PAYS

Parents (Cameroon + Francophone Africa first) — they buy confidence, structure,
and measurable progress toward exams and school success.

WHO USES

Students daily. Tutors as the intervention layer. Schools and partners later.

WHY NOW

LLMs make notes-to-interactive-content cheap. PrepSkul already has sessions,
games, and a tutor network — a rare combination vs pure revision apps or pure
marketplaces.

CORE DEFINITIONS (use these in Q&A)
================================================================================

"Bring learning to every learner's level" means:

  • Start from real capability, not only class label
  • Respect the learner's goal (exam, catch-up, skill, curiosity)
  • Choose the right modality (drill, game, path, live tutor)
  • Keep updating the path as they practice — living estimate, not static profile

Three types of "level" (do not conflate):

  1. ENROLLED LEVEL — what school/system expects (e.g. Form 2, Term 3)
  2. CAPABILITY LEVEL — what they can actually do today (reading, fractions,
     subject mastery)
  3. GOAL LEVEL — where they want to be (pass GCE, catch up, build confidence)

Three signals we use to understand learners:

  1. DECLARED — onboarding, parent profile, goals, pain points
  2. OBSERVED — SkulMate performance, mastery scores, session behavior
  3. HUMAN — tutor session notes, summaries, parent feedback

We are Bayesian, not oracle: onboarding is the starting hypothesis; sessions
confirm or correct it.

INVESTOR FAQ — HARD QUESTIONS & ANSWERS
================================================================================

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Q1. What do you mean by "every learner's level"? Isn't that just marketing?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

No. It is a product architecture choice.

"Level" is not one checkbox. We separate enrolled level (grade), capability
level (what they can do), and goal level (what they're working toward). Content
and routing follow capability + goal — grade is context, not destiny.

We serve exam students, school catch-up, university prep, skills, and open
revision. The system adapts to the individual, not a single demographic edge case.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Q2. How do you understand the learner's level?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Three signals, combined:

  DECLARED (onboarding / parent profile)
    Class, exam track, subjects, confidence, challenges, goals, learning path.

  OBSERVED (every SkulMate session)
    Accuracy by topic, mastery score, weak streaks, time on task, retries,
    which question types fail repeatedly.

  HUMAN (tutor layer)
    Session summaries, tutor notes, live assessment for gaps software should
    not pretend to fully diagnose.

Onboarding alone does not "know" their level. It gives us a prior. The first
few sessions plus tutor touchpoints turn that into a working estimate we keep
updating.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Q3. What sample questions do you ask? How do you know it's really their level?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

SAMPLE ONBOARDING QUESTIONS (hypothesis builders — not proof):

  Context (not capability proof)
    • What class or program are you in?
    • What exam or goal are you working toward, if any?
    • Which subjects matter most right now?

  Friction / self-reported gaps
    • In [subject], what usually breaks first?
      — I don't understand the question
      — I forget the steps
      — I make careless mistakes
      — I run out of time
    • How confident do you feel in reading / maths / science? (1–5)
    • What feels "too hard" even when school says you should know it?

  Destination
    • In the next 4–8 weeks, what would "better" look like?
    • Catch up, keep up, or get ahead?

  Modality
    • Do you already have a tutor?
    • Short games, step-by-step explanation, or live help?

  Placement-lite (roadmap — light diagnostics, not a full psychometric battery)
    • 60-second reading passage + 2 comprehension questions
    • 3 maths items with adaptive difficulty
    • Parent-assisted literacy check for younger learners where needed

HOW WE KNOW IT'S THEIR LEVEL (validation logic):

  1. Converging evidence — self-report + repeated failure on same skill type
  2. Prediction test — if reading is the blocker, simpler-text items should
     improve completion and accuracy
  3. Tutor confirmation — human validates high-stakes or foundational gaps
  4. Improvement signal — targeted practice moves metrics in 2–4 weeks if
     our estimate was right

We do not claim three signup questions replace assessment. We claim the system
gets smarter with every session.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Q4. Is this static onboarding or a dynamic system?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Both today — honestly.

DYNAMIC (live now):
  • AI generation calibrated by learner context (class, goals, subjects)
  • Per-topic mastery updates after every game session
  • Weak-topic detection and gated resurfacing on home
  • Adapted explanations when learners struggle (style rotation)
  • Tutor escalation when AI practice isn't enough
  • Parent progress view with weak topics and session highlights

BUILDING (roadmap):
  • Visible learner "you are here" map
  • Step-by-step Path UI
  • Scroll feed + spaced repetition ("what to review today")
  • Fuller placement diagnostics at onboarding

Pitch line: "The adaptive engine is real. The navigation UI is catching up.
We're not selling a survey — we're selling a loop that recalculates."


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Q5. What metrics do you track?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

CAPABILITY (is our level estimate right?)
  • Accuracy by topic and question type
  • Mastery score and weak streak per concept
  • Retry rate, abandon rate, time on task
  • Gap between enrolled-level content difficulty and observed performance

PROGRESS (are they moving?)
  • Mastery delta per topic (week over week)
  • Sessions completed (≥5 min voluntary revision)
  • Topics moving from "needs work" to "solid"
  • Streak length and D7/D30 retention

ROUTING (is the path working?)
  • Weak-topic nudge → replay completion rate
  • Tutor booking after escalation
  • Post-tutor improvement on flagged topics
  • Parent engagement with progress view / digest

OUTCOMES (longer term)
  • Parent-reported confidence
  • Exam results where available (slow, partner-dependent)

North star (SkulMate): Weekly active learners with ≥3 self-initiated revision
sessions of ≥5 minutes each.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Q6. How is this different from Quizlet, Gizmo, or YouTube?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Pure revision apps give content and games. They rarely have:

  • Live verified tutors on the same platform
  • Session → revision loop (class/tutor summary → challenge)
  • Parent-facing progress tied to weak topics, not vanity metrics
  • Exam/school context (Cameroon GCE, WAEC, BEPC) as background signal
  • Human escalation when AI hits a wall

PrepSkul is AI + tutors + adaptive routing — not AI alone, not tutors alone.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Q7. Don't you just teach to the test / exam prep only?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

No. Exam tracks are a strong wedge in our market (parents pay for structure),
but SkulMate supports open revision — notes, YouTube, skills, curiosity.

Curriculum alignment is background-only for learners: we never block off-syllabus
content or nag them with exam labels in the learner UI. Parents can see exam
context when it matches the child's profile.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Q8. What if the learner is far below grade level? Can AI fix that?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

AI is strong for practice, recall, and structured revision at the right
difficulty. It is not a replacement for a human when the gap is foundational
(literacy, severe catch-up, trauma, disability, complex motivation).

Our model: AI handles daily practice and routing signals; tutors handle
breakthrough and verification. We route up to humans when confidence is low or
performance pattern says software alone isn't working.

Illustrative example only: a learner enrolled in Form 2 who struggles with
reading would not be pushed into advanced chemistry drills. We'd route to
foundational practice and/or a tutor. That is one scenario — not our whole TAM.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Q9. How do tutors fit? Are you replacing them?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Tutors are the intervention layer, not the whole product.

  • AI handles repetition, recall, and between-session practice
  • Tutors handle misconceptions, motivation, accountability, and foundational
    gaps
  • SkulMate can generate post-session challenges from tutor summaries
  • Tutors see student weak topics instead of guessing what to cover

This makes tutors more effective and parents more willing to pay for both
subscription revision and live sessions.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Q10. What's your moat?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  1. Combined stack — revision AI + live tutoring + payments + scheduling in
     one product (rare in Africa edtech)
  2. Local curriculum context — GCE/WAEC/BEPC/MINESEC as background signal
  3. Engagement data flywheel — more sessions → better mastery estimates →
     better routing → better outcomes → more trust
  4. Parent trust layer — progress parents understand, not gamification theater
  5. Tutor network — supply side already on platform

Q14. Why Cameroon / Africa first?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  • Large out-of-school and extra-lessons market
  • High mobile penetration, painful fragmentation (WhatsApp tutors, PDF notes)
  • Exam culture (GCE, WAEC, BEPC) creates urgent parent willingness to pay
  • We understand local curriculum, payment (Mobile Money), and trust dynamics

Expansion: Francophone Africa → broader emerging markets with same pattern.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Q15. What's the biggest risk?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Be honest:

  1. Execution — adaptive UI must feel seamless, not bolted on
  2. Data cold start — need engagement before mastery signals are meaningful
  3. Quality — AI content must be good enough parents trust it
  4. Tutor supply quality — human layer must stay verified and consistent
  5. Distribution — parent acquisition cost in price-sensitive markets

Mitigation: engagement loops first (Phase A), prove parent progress view,
tutor escalation for AI limits, local curriculum depth.
